Inheriting a house in Missouri comes with a mix of emotions and practical challenges. You're dealing with loss while also facing decisions about a property you may not want to keep. Whether you inherited alone or with siblings, understanding your options and the process ahead can reduce stress and help you make a clear decision.

## The Probate Process in Missouri

When someone passes away, their estate typically goes through probate—the legal process of validating a will and distributing assets. In Missouri:

- **Formal probate** applies when there's a will or when the estate exceeds certain thresholds. It involves court supervision and can take several months.
- **Small estate procedures** may apply if the estate value is under roughly $40,000 (varies by county). This can simplify things.
- **No probate** is required if the property was held in a living trust, joint tenancy, or had a transfer-on-death deed.

Timelines vary. Simple cases might resolve in a few months; complex estates with multiple heirs or disputes can take a year or more. A probate attorney can advise on your specific situation.

## Your Options: List vs. Sell to an Investor

Once you have the legal right to sell (or are working with an executor who does), you have two main paths:

### Listing With a Real Estate Agent

Selling on the open market can yield a higher price—if the house is in good shape and the market is strong. You'll need to:

- Make repairs or disclose known issues
- Stage and show the property
- Pay agent commissions (typically 5–6% of the sale price)
- Wait for a buyer, inspections, and financing

This path works well when the property is in solid condition and you're not in a rush.

### Selling to an Investor (Cash Sale)

If the house needs work, is in a different city, or you want to close quickly, selling to a local investor may make more sense. Benefits include:

- No repairs or staging
- Fast closing (often 14–21 days)
- No agent fees
- A single buyer, fewer moving parts

The trade-off is typically a lower price than a fully renovated, listed sale—but you avoid the cost and hassle of repairs, holding costs, and agent commissions.

## Tax Considerations

Selling an inherited home can have tax implications:

- **Step-up in basis**: In most cases, your tax basis is "stepped up" to the fair market value at the date of death. That can reduce or eliminate capital gains tax if the property appreciated during the original owner's lifetime.
- **Estate tax**: Missouri does not have a state estate tax. Federal estate tax applies only to very large estates.
- **Consult a tax professional**: Inheritance and sale scenarios vary. A CPA or tax attorney can help you understand your specific situation.

## The Emotional Side

Letting go of a family home is hard. Many heirs feel guilt, nostalgia, or pressure from other family members. Some practical steps that help:

- Give yourself permission to sell if that's what makes sense for you.
- If co-heirs are involved, focus on shared goals: a fair process, clear numbers, and a timeline everyone can live with.
- Consider whether you'd rather invest time and money in the property or move forward with a clean break.
